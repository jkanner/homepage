N. Lake Ave Protected Bike Lanes

Let's make Lake Ave work for everyone

Pasadenaâ€™s Department of Transportation recently released its conceptual design for the North Lake Avenue Traffic and Pedestrian Safety Enhancement Plan. The plan calls for improved pedestrian safety on Lake Avenue, north of the 210.

The plan is a great start, but we believe it can be further improved by:
  • Adding protected bike lanes for safe, clean travel
  • Adding additional trees, to help make this a comfortable place to walk and ride
We believe these additions will:
  • Increase biking and walking in Pasadena
  • Increase access to the Lake Street metro station
  • Improve safety for the neighborhood
  • Increase business for neighborhood restaurants and shops
Providing safe places to bike or scooter helps everyone in the city. Increased biking leads to less traffic and less congestion, so there's more room on the road for motorists. Replacing short car trips with bikes or scooters can also have huge benefits for the rider, including improving health and saving money. For neighborhood residents, space for bikes means decreased traffic noise, safer streets, and a whole new way to explore and enjoy our beautiful city.

Read more about this proposal in the Pasadena CSC Blog Post.
